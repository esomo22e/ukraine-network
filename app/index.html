Unrest in Ukraine

February-March 2014

Ukrainian President, Viktor Yanukovych, and the Russian-leaning government is ousted from power during the popular uprising in the country. Followed by, Russia’s invasion and annexation of the Ukrainian peninsula of Crimea.

May 13, 2014

Hunter Biden, son of former-US VP Joe Biden, joins board of the Ukrainian energy company Burisma Holdings, which is owned by Mykola Zlochevskyl.

May 25, 2014

Petro Poroshenko was elected president of Ukraine.

Early 2015

Viktor Shokin becomes Ukraine’s prosecutor general. George Kent raises the issue about Hunter Biden’s work for Burisma and Joe Biden didn’t have the “bandwidth” to deal with the matter.

December 2015

The US, led by Joe Biden, tells Ukrainian leaders that the Obama administration would pull $1 billion in loan guarantees if it didn’t immediately fire Shokin.

March 2016

Shokin was dismissed from his position by Ukraine’s parliament.

November 2016

Trump is elected president, defeating Hilary Clinton.

Behind the Scenes- Trump Administration and Ukraine

May 2017

Robert Mueller was appointed to investigate Russian interference in the 2016 US Presidential election

October 27, 2017

Paul Manafort indicted as part of special counsel Robert Mueller’s investigation into Russian meddling in 2016.

April 19, 2018

Trump hired Giuliani as his personal lawyerer, initially focused on seeing out the Russia investigation

Late 2018

Giuliani speaks with Shokin, according to a complaint from an anonymous Whistleblower

Early 2019

Giuliani meets with Lutsenko and discuss Biden probe.

March 2019

Special counsel investigation ends and Mueller gives final report to Barr.

Bar releases report that Special Counsel did not establish collusion between the Trump campaign and Russia during the 2016 election.

Zelensky Election

April 21, 2019

Zelensky is elected president of Ukraine.

May 6, 2019

US Ambassador to Ukraine, Marie Yovanovitch stepped down following attacks from US and Ukraine officials.

May 9-11, 2019

The New York Times reports that Giuliani was planning to travel to Ukraine to discuss Biden probe and the Mueller investigation but was cancelled due to backlash.

May 20, 2019

Members of the Trump administration, Rick Perry, Kurt Volker, Gordon Sondland, Alexander Vindman, and Joseph Pennington goes to Zelensky inauguration.

The whistleblower says it was “made clear” to them that the President didn’t want to meet Zelensky until he saw how Zelenskyh “chose to act” in office.

June 12, 2019

In an interview with ABC News, Trump says he would accept help from a foreign government against his domestic political enemies if the occasion arose.
